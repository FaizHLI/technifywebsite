PAST PROJECTS

Olympic Gold Quest (OCQ) (2024)

Olympic Gold Quest is an Indian NGO helping underprivileged athletes in India pursue Olympic medals. OGQ currently supports around 400 athletes across 10 Olympic and 8 Paralympic sports. This includes approximately 150 junior athletes supported through the OGQ Junior Program and 101 para athletes supported through the OGQ Para Program. The project involved revamping OGQ's website, as their original website lacked integration between the front-end and back-end, as well as building a functional code-based admin panel and database.

Project Kayamanan (2024)

Project Kayamanan aims to raise the financial literacy of public high school students in the Philippines through online peer financial education. The project included adding responsiveness using media queries (CSS) and improving the user information tracking system.

Aquafit Intimate (2024)

Aquafit Intimate is an Israeli startup providing products and services that are designed to promote intimate well-being and health for women. The project involved building a mobile application with various features requested by Aquafit, with a focus on integrating and iterating Aquafit's conversational chatbot into the app.

The Lost Food Project in Malaysia (2021)

Technify partnered with The Lost Food Project in Malaysia to create a centralized donation application aimed at onboarding and engaging more potential food donors by streamlining the process of collecting food donations from neighborhood grocers.

PERWITI Soup Kitchen (2021)

PERWITI is an NGO in Malaysia that feeds the poor and homeless. Technify members had the opportunity to build a web application that recruits and onboards new volunteers while promoting the work of PERWITI soup kitchens in Kuala Lumpur. The web application provides live updates on soup kitchen operations for the general public and potential volunteers to view. It also includes backend controls for PERWITI to register volunteers. Through recruiting more volunteers, the organization is able to feed over 50% of the homeless population in Kuala Lumpur.
